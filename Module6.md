# Module 6 - Network Media

## What I learned

### Network media
Network media is the channel that carries data from a source to a destination.  
Modern networks mainly use copper, fiber-optic, or wireless media.

### Copper media
Copper cables carry data as electrical impulses.  
They are common in Ethernet networks and are often used in homes and offices.

### Twisted-pair cable
Twisted-pair is the most common network cable in local area networks.  
The wires are twisted together to reduce interference.

### Coaxial cable
Coaxial cable is a type of copper cable used by cable TV providers and some internet connections.  
It has a central copper core, insulation, shielding, and an outer jacket.

### Fiber-optic cable
Fiber-optic cable carries data as pulses of light.  
It supports very high speeds, long distances, and is not affected by electrical interference.

### Wireless media
Wireless transmission carries data through electromagnetic waves instead of cables.  
It is flexible and convenient, but can be affected by range and interference.

### Choosing network media
The choice of media depends on:
- maximum distance
- installation environment
- amount of data and speed required
- cost of the media and installation

### Media comparison
Fiber is often preferred for longer distances and higher bandwidth.  
Copper is common and practical for short local connections.  
Wireless is useful when mobility and convenience are important.

## Key terms

- **Network media** – the channel used to carry data  
- **Copper cable** – media that carries data as electrical impulses  
- **Twisted-pair** – common Ethernet cable made of twisted wire pairs  
- **Coaxial cable** – shielded copper cable used in cable and broadband systems  
- **Fiber-optic cable** – cable that carries data as light  
- **Wireless media** – data transmission through electromagnetic waves  
- **EMI/RFI** – electromagnetic and radio interference that can affect copper media  
- **Bandwidth** – the amount of data a medium can carry  
- **Cable run** – the maximum distance a cable can carry a signal effectively
