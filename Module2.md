# Module 2 - Network Components, Types, and Connections

## What I learned

### Clients and servers
Devices connected to a network can have different roles.  
A **client** asks for data or a service, and a **server** provides it.  
For example, a web browser is a client, and the website server sends back the page.

### Peer-to-peer
In a peer-to-peer (P2P) network, devices can share resources directly with each other.  
This is simple and cheap, but it is less secure, harder to manage, and does not scale well.

### Network components
A network is made of three main parts:
- **end devices** – devices people actually use, like PCs, phones, printers, and cameras
- **intermediary devices** – devices that help move and control traffic, like routers, switches, and firewalls
- **network media** – the path the data uses, like cables or wireless signals

### End devices
End devices are the source or destination of data.  
They are the devices users interact with directly, such as laptops, smartphones, tablets, printers, and servers.

### ISP connectivity
An **ISP** (Internet Service Provider) is the company that connects a home or business network to the internet.  
Examples include cable providers, phone providers, and mobile providers.

### Ways to connect to the internet
The most common home internet connections are:
- **Cable**
- **DSL**

Other possible options include:
- **Cellular**
- **Satellite**
- **Dial-up** (older and much slower)

### Router and modem
A home network usually connects to the internet through a router.  
The router helps multiple devices connect to the internet and also provides local networking, IP addressing, and basic security.

## Key terms

- **Client** – a device or program that requests data or services  
- **Server** – a device or program that provides data or services  
- **Peer-to-peer (P2P)** – a network where devices can share directly with each other  
- **End device** – a user device that sends or receives data  
- **Intermediary device** – a network device that moves or controls traffic  
- **Network media** – the physical or wireless path data travels through  
- **ISP** – the company that gives internet access  
- **Cable** – internet delivered through cable TV infrastructure  
- **DSL** – internet delivered over land-line telephone wires  
- **Cellular** – internet through a mobile network  
- **Satellite** – internet delivered through satellite communication  
- **Dial-up** – old telephone-based internet connection  
- **Router** – device that connects local devices to other networks, usually the internet  
- **Modem** – device that connects your router or computer to the service provider
